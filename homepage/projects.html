Criteria: In “City Limits,” Colson Whitehead explores how each New Yorker has his or her own private New York built brick by brick from experiences of interacting in the city. He concludes that we must cherish the places of our private New York because they are like landmarks to our identities as we grow and change with the city; this city has helped create each one of us, and the city and our “selves” are inextricably intertwined. So here we are - each of us will write our own homage to a part of our own New York, an aspect of the city that we will always carry with us. The purpose of this project is to develop a central idea about YOUR New York City: either a specific observation about life in the city, or a specific observation about New Yorkers or a subset of New Yorkers, or an homage to a particular community within the city, a window or sliding glass door for others to better appreciate this city. Here's my essay!
